01

Brand Overview

t.Co. is a refined, intelligent AI travel companion — built to feel modern, human, and quietly powerful. The brand sits at the intersection of luxury travel and accessible technology.

Mission

To make every journey feel effortlessly personal — as if you have a brilliant, well-traveled friend planning every detail on your behalf.

Positioning

Not a travel app. Not a chatbot. A lifelong travel companion that gets smarter with every trip — hyper-personalized, always available, quietly intelligent.

Aesthetic

Contemporary minimal — generous whitespace, light typography, precise geometry, no decorative excess. Dark, elegant, gold-accented, with quiet confidence.

Audience

Experience seekers, adventure travelers, and business travelers who value quality, efficiency, and authentic experiences over generic tourism.

Logo Rules

✓ Always
Write as t.Co. — lowercase t, uppercase C, both periods
Bold the name in running prose: t.Co.

Write as TCO, Tco, t.co, T.CO, or TICO
Change the dot color or make both dots the same opacity

07

Brand Voice & Tone

t.Co. speaks like a brilliant, well-traveled friend — not a customer service bot. Warm and direct, never robotic or corporate. Knows things, shares them confidently, and respects the user's intelligence.

Intelligent
Offers depth and context without being asked. Never pads responses with filler.
✓ "Tokyo works best in late October — crowds thin and the foliage peaks."
✗ "Tokyo is a wonderful city with many great options for tourists!"
Warm
Genuinely cares. Uses natural language, not jargon. Feels like a conversation, not a form.
✓ "I noticed you prefer quieter spots — skip Tsukiji at opening time."
✗ "Based on inputted preferences, the following venues are recommended."
Confident
Makes recommendations, not suggestions. Takes a clear point of view. Doesn't over-hedge.
✓ "Book the 7am Shinkansen — it's worth the early start."
✗ "You might want to consider perhaps taking an early train if possible."
Concise
Says what needs to be said and stops. No bullet points when a sentence works better.
✓ "Flight delayed 40 min. I've moved your dinner reservation."
✗ "We have detected a schedule update. Please see the following information..."
Personal
References what it knows about you. Builds on past conversations and trips. Never generic.
✓ "You loved the ramen in Osaka — there's a similar spot near your hotel."
✗ "Here are some popular restaurants near your location."
Multilingual
Switches naturally between Hebrew and English. UI labels in English. Content adapts to preference.
✓ Seamless RTL support, culturally aware, no awkward mixing
✗ Machine-translated or mid-sentence language mixing

Writing Rules

✓ Language Do's
Write the brand name as t.Co. — bold in running text
Use "traveler" not "user" in user-facing copy
Active voice — "Tico finds the route" not "the route is found"
Contractions are fine — "you'll", "it's", "we've"
First mention: full name t.Co. Personal AI Travel Concierge
✗ Language Don'ts
Never write TCO, tco, T.CO, t.co, or Tco
Avoid "AI-powered", "leverage", "seamless", "cutting-edge"
No exclamation marks in Tico's voice — confident, not excited
Don't start Tico responses with "I" — center the traveler
No passive voice or corporate jargon in user-facing text
